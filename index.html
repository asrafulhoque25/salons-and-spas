The real problems

8 reasons most salon and spa websites quietly hand bookings to Vagaro

Service menu buried three taps deep

The average salon visitor decides within 14 seconds whether your site is worth their time. If your nail extension price isn't visible without tapping 'Services → Nails → Extensions,' you've already lost to the salon whose Google listing showed pricing right in the snippet.

Booking widget is a third-party iframe

Most salons hand booking to Vagaro or Booksy and embed a 600px iframe that breaks mobile and dilutes branding. The booking widget should feel native to your site, not like an ad for the scheduling platform you pay $30 a month to use.

No transparent pricing — and prospects bounce to find it

'Call for pricing' is dead. Beauty consumers expect to see at least a starting price before they invest emotional energy in choosing your salon. Without it, you lose them to the salon that publishes a menu with ranges.

Stylist portfolios live on Instagram, not your site

Your best work is on someone else's platform earning them ad revenue. Google can't index Instagram captions reliably, so a search for 'balayage Phoenix' will never surface that photo your stylist tagged six months ago.

One 'Services' page where you need eight

If 'Balayage,' 'Brazilian Blowout,' and 'Color Correction' share one URL, none of them rank. Each service is a separate search intent and deserves a dedicated page targeting that specific keyword.

Photo galleries take 8 seconds to load on mobile

Beauty is a visual category, but unoptimized galleries kill mobile conversion. Every extra second of load time loses you another 7% of the visitors you paid (in time and SEO) to attract.

No reviews showing on the site itself

You have 4.9 stars on Google, but your homepage shows zero reviews. Visitors won't open a new tab to check Yelp — they'll just leave. Reviews need to live where the decision is being made.

No gift card purchase flow

Gift cards are 18 to 28 percent of December salon revenue at well-run shops. Sites without an online gift card flow send those buyers to your competitor whose 'Buy a Gift Card' button is in the header year-round.

8 features salon owners actually use (and most websites skip)

Stylist profile pages with personality and portfolio

Each stylist gets a real page — specialties, years of experience, training, and a gallery of their own work. Clients book a specific stylist 3x more often than 'next available.'

Before-and-after gallery with category filters

Google reviews and Yelp aggregated on every service page

Mobile-first tap-to-call and tap-to-book CTAs

Loyalty and membership signup built into the homepage

8 specializations, one playbook

Website design built around your specific salon or spa niche

A hair salon and a med spa sell to entirely different buyers in entirely different decision states. We design for the niche, not a generic 'salon' template.

Hair salon website design

Hair clients book around stylist and specialty — balayage, color correction, extensions, curly hair specialist. We design booking-first homepages with stylist portfolios, transparent service menus, and before-and-after galleries that win the 'show me first' mental test most clients run before booking.

Nail salon website design

Nail clients are visual-first and price-sensitive. Service menus with starting prices, photo-heavy design galleries by nail type (acrylic, gel, dip, French, nail art), and easy online booking. Mobile speed matters here more than anywhere — clients scroll Instagram, then Google your salon, then decide in under a minute.

Med spa website design

Med spa prospects research treatments before they pick a provider. We build per-treatment pages for Botox, fillers, laser hair removal, microneedling, IPL, body contouring — each with realistic expectations, FAQ, and consultation booking. Includes the medical disclaimers and compliance language med spas need.

Day spa website design

Day spas sell relaxation and packages. Mood-driven imagery, package pricing prominent, gift cards in the header, and seasonal promotional flows. We design for the gift-giver and the self-care buyer — two distinct mindsets that need different conversion paths.

Barbershop website design

Barbershop clients want appointment booking and walk-in info, fast. Masculine aesthetic, hours and current wait time visible, barber profiles with portfolios. We design for the lunchtime decision-maker who wants a cut tonight and is comparing three shops on Google.

Lash and brow studio website design

Lash and brow clients pick a technician based on portfolio. Per-technician profile pages with specialties (classic, hybrid, volume, mega volume, brow lamination, microblading), before-and-after galleries, and consultation booking for higher-ticket services.

Massage therapy website design

Massage clients book by modality — Swedish, deep tissue, prenatal, sports, hot stone, lymphatic drainage. We design pages that explain each modality in plain English, integrate intake forms that meet practitioner standards, and route booking by therapist specialty.

Color specialist salon website design

Color specialists charge premium and need premium positioning. Detailed before-and-after color portfolios, color theory content that signals expertise, in-depth consultation booking, and pricing transparency for color correction (the highest-ticket service in most color salons).

Salon and spa website design in your city

Beauty is a local-intent industry. Google ranks salon results by proximity and Google Business Profile quality. We optimize every site for the specific city, neighborhood, and search behavior of your local market.

What we won't promise

SEO content with real keyword research

Page-1 rankings on day one for the most competitive keyword. 'Hair salon Manhattan' has been fought over for 15 years. We can reliably rank you for 'balayage Upper East Side' faster than the broad city term.

Online booking platforms beat your website

Outranking the booking platforms in directory results. Vagaro and Booksy will outrank your individual site for some 'salon [city]' queries — they have decades of SEO and millions of pages. We can win the local 3-pack and the specific service queries.

The site cannot book clients for you

Results without you using the site. The site brings the booking inquiries. Your team has to answer the phone, confirm the appointment, and provide the service that earns the review. We can't book the client for you.

Med spa requires compliance
